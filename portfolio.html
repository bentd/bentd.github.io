Project Case

This app was part of a startup venture I co-founded. We were selected to develop the business model and software as part of Domi Station's GetStarted startup incubator program (now defunct). The venture took the student award in the 2018 FL Startup Challenge.

The app created a marketplace for students to sell and buy used textbooks. The business model cut out university textbook stores as middle men. The idea was to make the used textbook market more profitable and accessible to university students. Revenue was generated via several sources: ads, sales fees, data aggregation, and an additional study guide marketplace.

The app was developed using the Ionic Framework (Mobile), Heroku + Flask (Backend), Clarifai (Censoring), among other frameworks.

Project ShellMaps

This app was a project for FIU's 2017 ShellHacks. It won the Women in Computer Science (WiCS) "breaking the glass ceiling" challenge (problem was to create a solution to help minorities).

The app was a map of minority-owned businesses in the Miami area.

The app was developed using Flask (Server Side Scripting) and DigitalOcean (Hosting Platform).

Project Shotgun

This app was a project I lead for FAMU's 2016 App Competition, finishing 3rd place. I pitched the idea to potential teammates and lead the technical and business development of the idea.

The app enables college and graduate students without transportation to carpool and plan rides home or other destinations during breaks. It would also allow car owners to give rides to common destinations and earn money while doing so.

The app was developed using PhoneGap (Mobile).
